# Module 2: The Threat Landscape

## Lesson 01: The Threat Landscape Overview

---

## Overview

A **Threat Landscape** is the collection of all existing and potential cybersecurity threats within a specific environment, industry, or domain. It includes information about the threats themselves and the individuals or groups responsible for carrying them out, known as **bad actors**.

As technology continues to evolve, new vulnerabilities and attack techniques emerge, making the threat landscape dynamic and constantly changing.

Understanding the threat landscape helps organizations identify risks, strengthen defenses, and respond effectively to cyberattacks.

---

## What is a Threat Landscape?

A threat landscape consists of:

* Known cybersecurity threats
* Potential future threats
* Attack methods used by cybercriminals
* Information about threat actors (bad actors)
* Emerging technologies and vulnerabilities

In cybersecurity, the threat landscape includes all threats that can affect computer systems, networks, applications, and data.

### Why is it Important?

Understanding the threat landscape helps organizations:

* Identify possible risks
* Reduce vulnerabilities
* Strengthen security controls
* Detect attacks faster
* Prepare effective incident response plans

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, you should be able to:

### 1. Identify Different Types of Bad Actors

Bad actors are individuals or groups that perform malicious activities against organizations or individuals.

Different attackers have different goals:

| Bad Actor Type      | Primary Motivation                 |
| ------------------- | ---------------------------------- |
| Cybercriminals      | Financial gain                     |
| Nation-State Actors | Espionage and state secrets        |
| Hacktivists         | Political or social causes         |
| Insider Threats     | Personal gain, revenge, negligence |
| Terrorist Groups    | Disruption and fear                |

### Example

* Hospitals are often targeted by cybercriminals seeking ransom payments.
* Government defense organizations are commonly targeted by nation-state actors interested in stealing classified information.

Understanding attacker motivations helps security professionals predict potential threats and implement stronger defenses.

---

### 2. Understand Attack Vectors and Cyber Threats

#### What is an Attack Vector?

An **Attack Vector** is the path or method used by an attacker to gain unauthorized access to a system.

Examples include:

* Phishing emails
* Malware infections
* Weak passwords
* Unpatched software
* Social engineering attacks

#### Why is Understanding Attack Vectors Important?

It helps organizations:

* Discover security weaknesses
* Reduce their attack surface
* Implement preventive controls
* Improve security awareness

---

### 3. Understand Threat Intelligence

#### What is Threat Intelligence?

Threat intelligence is information that helps organizations understand and respond to cyber threats.

For information to qualify as threat intelligence, it must be:

| Requirement | Meaning                                    |
| ----------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| Relevant    | Applicable to the organization             |
| Actionable  | Can be used to improve security            |
| Contextual  | Provides meaningful background information |

If information is missing any of these characteristics, it is generally not considered true threat intelligence.

### Important Note

Threat intelligence is situational.

What is useful intelligence for one organization may not be useful for another.

Example:

* A bank may consider information about banking malware highly valuable.
* A manufacturing company may not find the same information equally important.

---

### 4. Understand Cyberattack Frameworks

Attack frameworks help security professionals understand how cyberattacks are planned and executed.

They provide structured models that describe attacker behavior and attack stages.

#### Cyber Kill Chain

The Cyber Kill Chain describes the stages of a cyberattack from initial reconnaissance to achieving attacker objectives.

Benefits:

* Helps detect attacks early
* Improves defensive strategies
* Enables proactive security measures

#### MITRE ATT&CK Framework

MITRE ATT&CK is a globally recognized knowledge base of attacker tactics and techniques.

Benefits:

* Maps real-world attack behavior
* Supports threat hunting
* Improves incident response
* Provides valuable threat intelligence

Security professionals widely use MITRE ATT&CK to understand and defend against modern cyber threats.

## Key Takeaways

* A threat landscape includes all current and potential cybersecurity threats.
* Bad actors have different motivations and target different industries.
* Attack vectors are methods attackers use to gain access to systems.
* Threat intelligence must be relevant, actionable, and contextual.
* Frameworks like Cyber Kill Chain and MITRE ATT&CK help security professionals understand attacker behavior.
* Understanding threats improves an organization's ability to prevent and respond to attacks.
