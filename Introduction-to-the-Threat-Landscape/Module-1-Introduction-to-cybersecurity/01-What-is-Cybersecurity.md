# What is Cybersecurity?

**Cybersecurity** is the practice of protecting computer systems, networks, devices, and digital information from:

* Unauthorized access
* Data theft
* Data loss
* Damage
* Cyberattacks

Cybersecurity is not only about technology. It also involves the actions and behavior of people who use technology.

### Key Point

A secure system requires:

* Secure technology
* Secure processes
* Security-aware people

All three work together to reduce cyber risks.

---

# Goals of Cybersecurity

The primary goal of cybersecurity is to protect information by maintaining:

| Principle       | Description                                             |
| --------------- | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| Confidentiality | Information is accessible only to authorized users.     |
| Integrity       | Information remains accurate and unaltered.             |
| Availability    | Information and services remain accessible when needed. |

These three principles form the foundation of information security and are commonly known as the **CIA Triad**.

---

# Role of Cybersecurity Professionals

Cybersecurity professionals help protect:

* Servers
* End-user devices (endpoints)
* Databases
* Networks
* Applications

Their responsibilities include:

* Identifying vulnerabilities
* Finding security gaps
* Detecting misconfigurations
* Implementing security controls
* Monitoring for threats

---

# Major Categories of Cybersecurity

Cybersecurity can be divided into several areas.

## 1. Critical Infrastructure Security

Protects essential services such as:

* Power grids
* Water systems
* Transportation systems
* Gas pipelines

A successful attack on critical infrastructure can impact entire communities.

---

## 2. Application Security

Protects software and applications from vulnerabilities and attacks.

Examples:

* Secure coding practices
* Software updates
* Vulnerability testing

---

## 3. Network Security

Protects networks and data traveling across them.

Examples:

* Firewalls
* Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS)
* Intrusion Prevention Systems (IPS)
* Network monitoring

---

## 4. Cloud Security

Protects data, applications, and services hosted in cloud environments.

Examples:

* Access controls
* Encryption
* Cloud monitoring

---

## 5. Internet of Things (IoT) Security

Protects connected devices such as:

* Smart cameras
* Smart TVs
* Printers
* Sensors
* Smart home devices

These devices often become targets because they may have weak security settings.

---

# Why Cybersecurity is Important

Modern life depends heavily on digital technologies.

Examples include:

* Online banking
* E-commerce
* Remote work
* Cloud storage
* Healthcare systems
* Government services

Cybersecurity helps ensure these services remain safe, reliable, and available.

### Example

A doctor in a remote location can securely share patient information with medical specialists across the world. Without cybersecurity, sensitive medical data could be exposed or altered.

# What is Information Security (InfoSec)?

**Information Security (InfoSec)** is the practice of protecting information regardless of its form.

Information can exist as:

* Digital files
* Printed documents
* Emails
* Contracts
* Policies
* Physical records

InfoSec focuses on:

* Preventing unauthorized access
* Detecting threats
* Responding to incidents
* Protecting sensitive information

---

# Examples of Information That May Need Protection

Organizations store many types of valuable information, including:

* Customer data
* Employee records
* Financial information
* Legal contracts
* Business plans
* Intellectual property

Not all information requires the same level of protection.

---

# Information Classification

Organizations classify information according to its sensitivity and importance.

| Classification | Description                                                   |
| -------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Unprotected    | Information that requires little or no protection.            |
| Protected      | Information requiring security controls.                      |
| Confidential   | Sensitive information requiring strong protection.            |
| Secret         | Highly sensitive information with strict access controls.     |
| Top Secret     | Extremely sensitive information requiring maximum protection. |

### Important Concept

The more critical the information, the stronger the security controls required.

---

# What is Information Systems Security?

**Information Systems Security** focuses on protecting information systems from:

* Unauthorized access
* Modification
* Destruction
* Service disruption
* Denial of access

Information systems include:

* Computers
* Servers
* Networks
* Storage devices
* Data centers
* Physical locations containing sensitive information

Information may be stored in either digital or physical form.

---

# Relationship Between the Three Concepts

The three concepts are closely related.

```text
Information Security (InfoSec)
        │
        └── Information Systems Security
                    │
                    └── Cybersecurity
```

### Understanding the Relationship

| Concept                      | Scope                                                     |
| ---------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------- |
| Information Security         | Protects all forms of information (digital and physical). |
| Information Systems Security | Protects systems that store or process information.       |
| Cybersecurity                | Protects digital assets and systems from cyber threats.   |

---

# The First Line of Defense Against Cyberattacks

Technology alone cannot stop cyberattacks.

Studies consistently show that **human error** is one of the leading causes of security breaches.

Examples include:

* Clicking malicious links
* Opening suspicious email attachments
* Using weak passwords
* Sharing sensitive information

Therefore, people are considered the first line of defense.

---

# Security Awareness and Education

Organizations should educate users to:

* Think before clicking links
* Identify phishing attempts
* Recognize suspicious emails
* Follow security policies
* Report unusual activities

Security awareness reduces the likelihood of successful attacks.

---

# Disaster Recovery and Backups

Another important defense strategy is preparing for unexpected incidents.

Organizations should:

* Perform regular backups
* Store backups securely
* Keep backup copies offline when possible
* Test backup restoration procedures

### Why Backups Matter

Backups help recover data after:

* Hardware failures
* Malware infections
* Ransomware attacks
* Accidental deletion
* Natural disasters

A good backup strategy minimizes downtime and data loss.

---

# Key Takeaways

* Cybersecurity protects networks, systems, devices, and digital information.
* Cybersecurity depends on technology, processes, and people.
* The CIA Triad consists of Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability.
* Information Security (InfoSec) protects information in all forms.
* Information Systems Security protects the systems that store and process information.
* Cybersecurity is a subset of Information Systems Security.
* Human error is one of the biggest causes of security incidents.
* Security awareness training is a critical defense mechanism.
* Regular backups are essential for disaster recovery and ransomware protection.
